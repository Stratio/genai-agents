# Modern Product

Contemporary tech-product visual language: indigo primary, an amber
accent for emphasis, rounded surfaces and a geometric sans throughout.
Built for product launch decks, feature announcements, pitch material
and internal product documentation that needs a confident contemporary
voice.

## Color palette (core)

| Token | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| primary | #6366f1 | headers, feature callouts, brand hits |
| ink | #0f172a | body text |
| muted | #64748b | secondary labels, captions |
| rule | #e2e8f0 | dividers, card borders |
| bg | #ffffff | page / main surface |
| bg_alt | #f8fafc | section backgrounds, cards |
| accent | #f59e0b | the one warm pop against the cool primary |
| state_ok | #10b981 | success, completed states |
| state_warn | #f59e0b | alerts (same hue as accent; tune by context) |
| state_danger | #ef4444 | critical errors |

## Optional extensions

- **Motion budget**: `expressive` (product decks can afford deliberate
  motion: staged reveals, smooth transitions, animated metrics)
- **Border radius**: `12px` (characteristically rounded surfaces)
- **Dark mode variant**: `bg` = `#0f172a`, `ink` = `#e2e8f0`,
  `primary` = `#818cf8`, `accent` = `#fbbf24`
- **Chart sequential**: base color `#6366f1`

## Tone family

`technical-minimal` (with warmer, more expressive dial settings).

## Best used for

- Product launch decks and pitch presentations
- SaaS marketing landing pages and feature announcements
- Internal product-team documents (PRDs, roadmaps, retros) that need
  a polished voice

## Anti-patterns

- Do not use for legal, compliance or audit documents — the tone is
  too marketing-forward.
- Do not combine with serif headlines; the geometric sans is the voice.
- Do not crowd the palette by adding more saturated hues into the chart
  categorical — the existing six already push the upper bound.
